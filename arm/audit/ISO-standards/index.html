Why adopt ISO standards?

The main benefits of adopting ISO standards are:

  • a structured approach for reviewing and improving business performance
  • improved and consistent approach for managing risk and delivering our products and services
  • increased focus on seeking and acting on customer feedback
  • enabling us to embed continuous improvement into all activities across the organisation

Achieving ISO certification is one way of demonstrating your commitment to improvement to the organisation, your customers and stakeholders.

How can we help you?

We can help you become certified for, or compliant with

ISO 13485 Quality management system for Medical devices
ISO 9001 The foundation for good business management
ISO 27001 Information security management
ISO 20000 IT service management

Choose the right standard

The starting point is choosing the most appropriate standard for your team: ​​

  • ensure your products or services meet customer requirements and are continually improved - ISO 9001

  • ensure you secure information assets such as financial information, and employee details - ISO 27001

  • ensure your managed services have controls in place to measure and maintain consistent levels of service - ISO 20000

Overview of our ISO process

1 Expression of interest

The first step is to contact us if you're interested in ISO standards. 

We engage with you to discover your needs, and arrange an awareness session to explain how certification can help you. You'll learn whether certification is right for your team.

 

2 Self assessment

We can help you complete a self-assessment questionnaire​ for each of the standards we support. This gives you a good idea of what you are already doing well and where you think you could do better. 

Your next step is to decide how you can address the identified gaps from the self-assessment, this is your improvement plan.  Your plan will include quality objectives and actions needed to meet your customer and stakeholder needs.  ​Some actions will involve influencing other teams so that the products and services they provide align with your own.

Your improvement plan is a living document and is under continuous review.  It takes into account progress against actions, new requirements and lessons learned. We will provide advice and support to you throughout this stage.

3 Baseline assessment

We provide an independent review of your working practices against the ISO standard.   

The assessment team spend time with your team to look at how well your ways of working conform to the standard.  We'll prepare a report detailing areas of good practice and where you need to improve.  You'll find out how close you are to demonstrating that you meet the requirements of the standard.

4 Pre-certification audit

Once you've made the changes, we provide you with a second assessment.  This will focus on the areas of focus from the baseline assessment report.  The assessment team will provide a report, agree an action plan and work with you to check progress.

The Quality council use the report to decide whether to add you to the scope of the certificate.

5 Quality council approval

Once the council agrees you are included in the certificate scope, you'll be assessed at the next visit by our external auditor. This usually happens in February and August.

6 External certification audit

The assessment will be about half a day in length and will focus on your process and guidance documentation.

You will receive a report with any non-conformities, and observations for improvement. We'll help you to ensure that you take corrective action within the agreed timescales.

 

7 Certification

You are issued with a certificate.

8 Routine audit

We'll provide you with on-going assessments and support as a part of our overall assurance activities.

9 External surveillance

The certification body will continue to monitor you with a surveillance visit every six to twelve months.  

What's the audit experience like?

As always, the Management Systems team and assessor are professional in their assessment and helpful in providing guidance and advice to help us improve customer service provision.

The audit provided an excellent opportunity for us to identify our gaps and provide reassurances as to the robustness of our existing processes. Whilst some of the findings we were aware of, even those provide us with a focus and an impetus to get on with correcting our areas which wouldn't have existed without the audit.

Audit identified specific areas for improvement to aid the teams preparation for ISO 9001. These have given the team specific focus to take forward as part of our improvement plan and preparation work.
