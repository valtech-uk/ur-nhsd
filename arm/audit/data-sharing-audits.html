Data sharing audit

We provide assurance that NHS Digital handles data appropriately and protects it. The audits contribute to mitigating the risk of failure to protect our data.

We carry out independent audits to ensure our customer that receive health data abide by the terms and conditions set by NHS Digital and the data is kept safe and secure.

  • We have the specialist knowledge and expertise to:
  • conduct audits at external organisations to ensure compliance with contractual requirements
  • cover key areas such as access control, information transfer, data destruction, operational management plus use and benefits
  • identify requirements that have not been met and opportunities for improvements
  • produce reports and guidance for NHS Digital senior management

You can see our findings published on the Data Sharing Audit section of NHS Digital website.

Why do we do these audits?

Our aim is to provide NHS Digital and the general public with assurance that data disseminated by Data Access Request Service to external organisations is handled appropriately and protected.

To provide transparency and greater awareness of how organisations are safeguard public health data we openly publish our reports.

There are approximately 900 data sharing access requests a year, and of those, a risk-based sample is selected to audit each year.

Audit process

The process has these main stages

1. Selection

If your team is a data recipient, you can be selected for audit for these reasons:

  • to seek confidence in your ability to handle data securely

  • when you are in possession of data supplied by NHS Digital

  • following destruction of the supplied data.

2. Notification

You receive a notification letter by email 10 working days before the proposed audit date.

For an unannounced heightened concern audit, there is no notification letter and no prior notice.

3. Supply of documents

You will need to supply documents covering

  • Key organisation aspects and people

  • Information transfer

  • Access control

  • Data destruction

  • Information governance

  • Use and benefits of the data

  • Risk management

4. Review of evidence

This is with your representative either onsite or via conference call

5. Findings

The Auditors share identified findings with the team.

Following review, we classify findings as one of the following:

  • Agreement nonconformity

  • Organisation nonconformity

  • Observation

  • Opportunity for improvement

  • Follow-up

A risk statement is then shared along with an action plan.

6. Publication

The report is published to the data sharing audit area of the NHS Digital website.

7. Review

Where appropriate, the Audit Team conduct post audit reviews to ensure organisations address their findings

Read the Data sharing audit guide for full details.

How is the audit service performing?

We request feedback on our audits to ensure we are delivering an effective service. We have been consistently given a high rating for the overall audit service.
