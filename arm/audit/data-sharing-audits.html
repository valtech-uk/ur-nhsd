Data sharing audit

We carry out independent audits to ensure our customer that receive health data abide by the terms and conditions set by NHS Digital and the data is kept safe and secure.

Our team of qualified auditors carry out data sharing audits on external organisations.

We have the specialist knowledge and expertise to:

  • cover key areas such as access control, information transfer, data destruction, operational management, risk management plus use and benefits
  • identify requirements that have not been met and opportunities for improvements

  • produce audit reports available to all stakeholders including the public

  • provide guidance for NHS Digital senior management

You can see our reports published on the Data Sharing Audit section of the NHS Digital website.

Why do we do these audits?

Our aim is to provide NHS Digital and the general public with assurance that data disseminated by the Data Access Request Service to external organisations is handled appropriately and protected.

To provide transparency and greater awareness of how organisations are safeguarding public health data, we openly publish our audit reports.

There are approximately 900 data sharing access requests a year, and of those, a risk-based sample is selected to audit each year.

Audit process

The process has these main stages

1. Selection

A data recipient can be selected for audit by DARS: 

  • to seek confidence in its ability to handle data securely prior to the receipt of data 
  • whilst in the possession of data supplied by NHS Digital 
  • following destruction of the supplied data

The Audit Team is then commissioned by DARS to audit the selected data recipients.

The Audit Team may also independently identify a data recipient from available data dissemination lists. Such audits would be confirmed with DARS and follow the same process described below. 

2. Notification

The data recipient will receive a notification letter by email at least 10 working days before the proposed audit date. 

Included with the letter will be a draft Audit Plan and a copy of the latest Audit Guide.

A few days after the data recipient has received the letter we will have a telephone call with them to explain the audit process and answer any initial questions

For an unannounced heightened concern audit, there is no notification letter and no prior notice.

A data sharing audit will fall under one of the following four types: 

  1. Routine 
  2. Focused 
  3. Pre-release 
  4. Heightened concern 
3. Supply of documents

The data recipient is asked to supply

  • key organisation aspects and people

  • information transfer

  • access control

  • data destruction

  • operational management and control

  • use and benefits of the data

  • risk management

4. Review of evidence

The Audit Team will conduct an evidence-based assessment of the data recipient’s processes and controls, and environment where data resides. The Audit Team will undertake interviews of relevant staff, review documents, records and uses of data along with an inspection of implemented controls. These interviews will either be onsite or via conference call.

The audit will also seek to determine whether the data recipient conforms to:

  • the issued NHS Digital data sharing framework contract and data sharing agreement
  • its own policies, processes and procedures
5. Findings

Following review, we classify findings as one of the following:

  • Agreement nonconformity

  • Organisation nonconformity

  • Observation

  • Opportunity for improvement

  • Follow-up

The Audit Team will produce a draft report which will be subject to several levels of review:

  • the data recipient, to check for factual accuracy and for commercially confidential and security sensitive information
  • selected internal teams

All factual inaccuracies will be corrected by the Audit Team. Other comments will be considered as appropriate. It is, however, important that the Audit Team acts independent from stakeholders.

Once all comments have been considered, the Audit Team will conduct a final quality assurance review before finalising the report.

6. Publication

The final report is published to the data sharing audit area of the NHS Digital website.

If the Audit Team needs to follow up the findings in a report, then an action plan will be sent to the data recipient for completion.

7. Review

Where appropriate, the Audit Team conduct post audit reviews to ensure organisations address their findings. In some cases more than one post audit  review is required to close an audit.

The post audit review reports are also published on the Internet. If the Audit Team needs to follow up the findings in a report, then an action plan will be sent to the data recipient for completion.

How are we performing?

We request feedback on our audits to ensure we are delivering an effective service. We have been consistently given a high rating for the overall audit service.

Some of the comments are:

Thank you so much for all your support help and advice along the way to get our systems in line with the requirements.

The audit was a very worthwhile experience and was an excellent learning opportunity.

The team were very professional and delivered the process as a way of collaboratively improving processes rather than an audit seeking to criticise an organisation.
