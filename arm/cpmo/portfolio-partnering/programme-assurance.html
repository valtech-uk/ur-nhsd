What is programme assurance?

Programme assurance is the set of actions necessary to provide confidence to senior leaders and stakeholders that work is controlled, on track to deliver and aligned with policy and strategy.

Assurance should be scheduled to provide decision makers with an assessment and outlook. It should be planned to minimise the impact on the team and reviewers.

Risk potential assessments

The RPA determines the degree of assurance required on a project or programme. The IPA owns the RPA template.

Programme assurance process

All programmes complete a risk potential assessment annually and at key decision points to detail the key risk areas. This assessment is used to determine the degree of assurance required on a project or programme.

The overall process at a high level is detailed here. For more detail, see the resources section.

Planning

The Senior Responsible Owner decides when to conduct a Risk Potential Assessment. Triggers include:

  • significant changes such as funding, scope, milestones or portfolio reprioritisation

  • when navigating functional controls or when passing significant milestones

  • Otherwise, review at least twice a year

Scoring

The score and project size affect the process. The second  is for programmes listed in the Government Major Projects Portfolio.
