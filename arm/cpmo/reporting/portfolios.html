Citizen Health Tech - NHS login

Creating a single process for people to securely and conveniently access any approved digital health and social care service, in a simple and consistent way.

NHS login is a service that has been created by the NHS for patients and the public. It provides a re-usable way for people to access multiple digital health and social care services with a single login, which includes authentication for returning users.  NHS login can be used by the public to securely access their health and care information wherever they see the NHS login button. People can use NHS login to prove who they are safely and securely online.

Digital services and tools can quickly integrate with NHS login via the integration toolkit, providing users with a recognised and reusable login to their services.

Citizen Health Tech – NHS.UK

NHS.uk is the UK’s biggest health website with more than 54 million visits per month. However, nhs.uk is more than just a website, it is the NHS national platform for hosting information, tools and services about health, illnesses, medical services, treatment and wellbeing, with associated channels for delivering these to patients, the public and medical practitioners. Along with GPs, it is now one of the principle points of entry for people to the NHS. The programme’s role is both operational and transformational. As well as supporting patients, their families and carers and the wider public with their health, illnesses and wellbeing today, it is also a major driver of the NHS Empower the Person ambition to get people to change their behaviours so that they take more control of their health and care.

Citizen Health Tech – The NHS App

Providing a simple and secure way for people to access a range of NHS services on their smartphone or tablet. The app is available to everyone in England aged 13 and over.

The NHS App enables people to access a range of healthcare services on their smartphone or tablet including check their symptoms, order repeat prescriptions, book appointments and register their organ donation decision. Latterly the NHS App has introduced important new services including some that support the COVID-19 response by enabling users to transact with the NHS from their homes.

This includes providing personal health record functionality including secondary care appointments; the ability to view and change the pharmacy that your prescription is sent to; the ability to send secure messages between practice and patient; and providing online consultation which enables practices to triage patients to the most appropriate point of care rather than making an appointment.

The NHS App integrates national local digital health and care services into a single, easy to use and standardised channel, to improve the citizen experience and to empower patients to take more control of their health and care.
