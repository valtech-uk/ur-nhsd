Sponsorship and vision

The Business Sponsor and the Business Visionary demonstrate clear and proactive ownership of the project

Senior business ownership is essential. Without this, a project is likely to be starved of essential business resources. This results in major issues that can't be dealt with by the project team remaining unresolved. Such unresolved issues either cause a project to stall or leave it exposed to high-risk assumptions or workarounds. A committed Business Sponsor who really cares about the project is almost always willing and able to push for significant issues to be resolved where senior management action is needed. This is essential to the success of the Programme/Project to ensure there is commitment to the availability of resource, knowledge and skills for the Programme/Project. The Business Visionary is responsible for making sure that all parts of the business impacted by the business vision understand and buy in to the vision and remain aligned with it. Managing business stakeholder expectation on a proactive and ongoing basis is key to the success of projects with a wide business impact.

The Business Vision driving the project is clearly stated and understood by all members of the project team

All day-to-day project decisions at all levels in the project should be checked against the business vision. Even If the decision seems small or insignificant simply considering: "Does this help move us closer to achieving the business vision?". If the consensus is that the business vision is not clear and/or not understood, arranging a session for the Business Visionary to share his/her vision and answer any questions mitigates the risk of having a solution that is not aligned with the business vision

The Business Sponsor and Business Visionary understand that active business involvement is essential and have the willingness and authority to commit appropriate business resources to the project

The first and best course of action is to try to secure the necessary time. It is helpful to agree up front the amount of time and the level of commitment expected. This can be managed by:
  • Attendence at Daily Stand-ups whenever possible
  • Availability by phone
  • Availability for Teams Calls
Resources

Product Owners and Delivery Managers are allocated to the project at an appropriate and consistent level sufficient to fully support the Sprints

Where resources are only available part-time, try to secure formal agreements as to how many hours per day/week they will spend on the Programme/Project.

Where multiple team members are part-time, try to synchronise agreements in order to allow team members to work collaboratively.

Agree objectives and schedule the work to match availability of resources being careful not to overcommit, especially in circumstances when availability may be unpredictable. In cases where resource availability is very unpredictable, do not commit to a delivery date, just agree to try to meet a target.

Try to make the work as granular as possible: the smaller each piece of work, the more likely it will get finished within the timescale
