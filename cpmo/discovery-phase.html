What is the discovery phase?

Before you commit to building a service, you need to understand the problem that needs to be solved.  The required area to Discover is in the NWC.  That means learning about:

  • your users and what they’re trying to achieve
  • any constraints you’d face making changes to how the service is run - for example because of technology or legislation
  • the underlying policy intent you’ve been set up to address - this is the thing that government wants to change or make happen
  • opportunities to improve things - by sharing data with other teams, for example

What you learn in this phase is essential as it helps to indicate if you should move forward to the alpha phase or not. You shouldn’t start building your service in this phase either, that’ll come later

There’s no set time period for a discovery, but around 4 to 8 weeks is typical. The purpose of your discovery should dictate how long you spend on it. Your discovery is finished when you’ve decided if you want to move onto alpha or direct to private beta

What do I need to do?

  1. An Investment justification may be required or requested if the funding does not sufficiently cover your Alpha phase.

    You need to provide sufficient information to the Technology and Finance Performance Board (TFPB) enabling them to consider the case for funding approval

    Review business process

  2. Provide mandatory documentation, which may be contained in Confluence and Jira or similar tools
    • User Stories, Story Point Estimation & Sprint Plan  
    • Retrospective Review and Discovery Outputs   
    • Initial Roadmap/ Sprint schedule for next stages and key inter-dependencies
    • High level Burndown Charts  
    • Product Backlogs & Complexity estimate 
    • Draft Technical Spikes 
    • Risk / Issue Log & Risk Potential Assessment
    • Project Board Terms of Reference 
    • Project Definition  
    • Basic Highlight Report (dependency on reporting to define) 
    • Programme Initiation Document (PID) including delivery team responsibility & structure matrix
GDS spend controls

You will need GDS approval at the beginning of each agile phase before you can start any work.
